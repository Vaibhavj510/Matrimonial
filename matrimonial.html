A little about me.

I am a morning person by nature — the day starts early and quietly. By afternoon, the kitchen is where things happen. I cook when I want to take care of people, and the people in my life will tell you — the pav bhaji and chole are worth the trip. Friends have travelled across cities for a plate. I take that seriously.

I grew up in Pune, Maharashtra — but my roots are in the Western Ghats, where the monsoons set the calendar and summers smelled of ripe mangoes. That warmth stayed with me through Germany, through twenty-one countries and counting. Wherever I go, I find myself more interested in the people than the monuments. I want to know how they live, what they eat, what makes them laugh.

Vietnam remains my favourite country so far — the food, the culture, the people. Nothing comes close. I am vegetarian, which keeps things interesting across twenty-one countries, and I have learned that the best food in the world finds you if you are curious enough to look.

I carry a Nikon almost everywhere. Not to document, but because looking through a lens teaches you to notice what you'd otherwise walk past. I grew up playing tabla — school prayers, private ceremonies, the rhythm underneath things. I did theatre at university, served as General Secretary of my college. I have always liked being around people, building something together, making a room feel like home.

// what I am looking for

What I am looking for.

I am not looking for a mirror image of myself. I am looking for a person. Someone with her own education, her own opinions, her own way of seeing the world. Someone who has built something for herself — a career, a passion, a point of view — and knows what she wants from life. Her interests don't need to match mine. I am genuinely curious about people who see things differently, and I believe two people with their own inner worlds make a richer home together.

Germany is where my life is rooted. Eight years, a career I am proud of, permanent residency, a home I have built with care. I am looking for someone who is open to building her life here — whether that means continuing her education, starting her career, or simply being brave enough to begin something new. I know what that journey feels like. I made it myself.

What I can offer is a home that is warm, well-fed, full of music and conversation and the occasional spontaneous trip somewhere neither of us has been. A partner who will be genuinely taken care of. A family — here and in India — that will welcome her completely. And if she likes pav bhaji — she will be very well looked after.
